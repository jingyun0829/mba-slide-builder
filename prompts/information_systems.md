Information systems / MIS module emphasis:
- Frame IT investments as strategic decisions — business case, risk, governance — not technical details.
- Prefer examples from the last 24 months: AI adoption, cloud migrations, ERP failures, data platform deals, vendor lock-in events (Broadcom/VMware, Oracle price hikes), outsourcing shifts.
- Tensions worth planting: build vs buy vs borrow, speed vs compliance, core vs context, agile vs waterfall in regulated industries, vendor lock-in and switching costs.
- Name the canonical frameworks — SDLC, Agile, Porter's value chain, TCE, core vs context — but treat them as lenses, not doctrine.
- Good closing moves: give a concrete "the board is deciding next week" scenario and force students to commit to build, buy, or outsource before revealing what the company actually did.
