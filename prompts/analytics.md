Business analytics module emphasis:
- Tie every technique to a decision: what business problem does this solve, for which stakeholder, with what risk of being wrong?
- Recommend concrete tools by topic: Excel / SQL for foundations, Tableau / Power BI for visualization, Python (pandas, scikit-learn) or R for modeling, dbt / Snowflake for data engineering.
- Prefer real data sets and real company examples from the last 24 months: A/B tests at major tech firms, pricing experiments, churn modeling, marketing mix modeling, LLM-as-judge evaluation.
- Distinguish descriptive vs. predictive vs. prescriptive analytics — name the shift in decision authority each implies.
- Tensions worth planting: correlation vs causation (show a real confounded example), overfitting vs business interpretability, model accuracy vs fairness, dashboards vs decision-making.
- Good closing moves: hand students a partial output (chart, model summary, A/B result) and force them to recommend a business decision before showing the full data.
