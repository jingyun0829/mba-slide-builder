Statistics / quantitative methods module emphasis:
- Tie every method to a business decision — hypothesis tests mean "should we launch," regression means "what drives revenue," etc.
- Show at least one real data set or concrete numerical example per concept. Avoid pure formula lectures.
- Prefer examples from the last 24 months: A/B test disputes at major platforms, election polling, medical trial reporting, ML model fairness audits.
- Tensions worth planting: statistical significance vs business significance, p-hacking, confounding, survivorship bias, sample size vs decision urgency.
- Name tools explicitly (Excel, Python/scipy, R) and which is appropriate when.
- Good closing moves: give a partial result (p-value, confidence interval, regression output) and ask students to defend a business recommendation under uncertainty.
